About this viewer

This tool visualizes three spatial responsibility metrics for wildfire defensible-space mitigation in a single wildland-urban-interface neighborhood:

  • PR — Personal Responsibility: the part of a homeowner's own defensible space that lies on their own parcel and doesn't overlap any neighbor's defensible space.
  • SR — Shared Responsibility: area where two neighbors' defensible space buffers overlap, attributed to whichever parcel the overlap physically sits on.
  • OR — Owed Responsibility: area on a homeowner's own parcel that is only reachable by a neighbor's defensible space buffer (not their own) — mitigation the homeowner owes because a neighbor's buffer happens to reach their land. Directed edges point toward whoever owes.

Each metric is reported both as raw geometric area [ft²] and as area-weighted responsibility [ft³/min] (area × mean wildfire hazard).

How to use it:

  • View: switch between the full GIS map (parcels, buildings, defensible-space buffers, PR/SR/OR region shapes) and a simplified network graph.
  • Active network: choose SR or OR — controls both which directed links are drawn and which network the mitigation simulation runs on.
  • Choropleth layers: toggle PR/SR/OR region shading on or off independently, and switch between area and responsibility units.
  • Click any building or node to see its stats and highlight its SR/OR connections.
  • Mitigation simulation: pick a strategy (random, localized, targeted) and drag the slider to progressively remove links, tracking remaining total responsibility and active sub-networks.
